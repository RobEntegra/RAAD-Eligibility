Question 1 of 8
What type of livestock operation do you run?
The current RAAD round is focused on beef feedlot operations.
Question 2 of 8
Is your feedlot NFAS accredited?
National Feedlot Accreditation Scheme (NFAS) accreditation is required to participate in the RAAD program.
Question 3 of 8
How many head does your feedlot typically run?
A 2,000 head project over 2 years can attract up to $1.8M in MLA co-funding.
Question 4 of 8
Does your feedlot currently have shade or shelter infrastructure?
MLA gives higher priority to feedlots with no existing shade or shelter — this is one of the strongest eligibility signals.
Question 5 of 8
Are you open to trialling a new or novel shade/shelter design?
RAAD favours fresh approaches — assessors prioritise projects that offer something new to the industry.
Question 6 of 8
Can you provide full feedlot closeout data?
This includes animal performance, carcase grading and economic records — a core requirement for both Gold and Silver Standard.
Question 7 of 8
Would you share your research results openly with the broader feedlot industry?
This is a key assessment criterion — participants are expected to contribute learnings so the whole industry benefits.
Question 8 of 8
Which research tier suits your operation?
Gold Standard ($2.46/head/day) requires a randomised controlled trial and peer-reviewed publication. Silver Standard ($1.97/head/day) is a simpler commercial evaluation.
