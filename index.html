Question 1 of 10
What best describes your operation?
RAAD is open to a range of livestock operations — select what fits you best.
Question 2 of 10
Is your operation NFAS accredited?
NFAS accreditation is required for feedlot participants. Grazing and other operations may still be considered for novel project ideas.
Question 3 of 10
Roughly how many livestock do you run?
Larger operations attract more co-funding — a 2,000 head project over 2 years can attract up to $1.8M from MLA.
Question 4 of 10
Does your property currently have shade or shelter infrastructure?
MLA gives higher priority to operations with no existing shade or shelter — one of the strongest eligibility signals.
Question 5 of 10
Which of these do you have a question, challenge or idea around?
These are all areas RAAD can potentially fund — even if you haven't thought of them as R&D before.

Question 6 of 10
Is your project idea new or different to what's already been done?
RAAD prioritises fresh approaches. Novel ideas score highly with the assessment committee.
Question 7 of 10
Can you provide performance and economic data from your operation?
RAAD requires participants to collect and share animal performance, carcase grading and economic records during the project.
Question 8 of 10
Would you share your research results openly with the broader industry?
A key assessment criterion — participants are expected to contribute learnings so the whole industry benefits.
Question 9 of 10
Could you obtain regulatory and ethics approvals for an on-property research project?
All RAAD projects require animal ethics approval and relevant regulatory sign-off before commencement.
Question 10 of 10
How involved would you want to be in the research process?
Cattle: Gold Standard $2.46/head/day · Silver Standard $1.97/head/day. Sheep & lamb: Gold Standard $0.25/head/day · Silver Standard $0.20/head/day. Both tiers require a research protocol and data collection.

Note: This is a preliminary eligibility check only. Final eligibility is determined by MLA's independent assessment committee. All operations are encouraged to register their interest — Rob at Entegra can help you assess your options before you submit.
